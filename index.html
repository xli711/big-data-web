Parks for Affordable Housing


Subject of Study

This project examines whether public housing developments in the City of Philadelphia are located close to desirable public amenities compared to regular housing developments. We specifically study public green spaces since they are proved to be closely related with quality of life and land values[1].


Data and Method

We explore the usage of social media data to measure activity level and perception of public green spaces, instead of the more objective and traditional network or gravity accessibility measurements. The count of Twitter and Instagram data at each public green space is used to measure its activity level, and the emojis contained in these posts are used to measure the public's perception and attitudes towards each park[2].
